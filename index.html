Australia's Wealth Distribution Maps

This map visualisation shows wealth distribution across Australia, highlighting states with the highest average taxable incomes. New South Wales and Victoria emerge as the wealthiest states, with concentrated wealth around major urban centres, particularly in Sydney and Melbourne. Queensland and Western Australia also show notable income clusters, though wealth is less centralised compared to NSW and VIC.

Average Salary vs Superannuation

This bar chart examines the relationship between average salary and superannuation balances across regions in VIC and NSW. High-income regions, predictably, have greater super balances, suggesting a direct relationship between income and retirement savings. Outliers like Melbourne's inner suburbs have lower super balances relative to income, indicating potential lifestyle expenditure affecting long-term savings.

Average Taxable Income vs Net Tax Paid

The scatter plot shows the relationship between average taxable income and net tax paid across various postcodes. Higher-income regions contribute proportionally more in tax. However, regions such as those in Sydney's north shore contribute notably more, suggesting that tax contributions scale with income but vary by postcode due to factors like deductions and net tax adjustments.
